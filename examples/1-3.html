Amy Tsai is a designer for film and theatre based in New York City and Taiwan. Her works have been selected by organizations like Athena Film Festival and Brooklyn Youth Arts Expo. Her design often features an interdisciplinary approach in various design and fine art areas. She believes that production design goes beyond location, period and style. It is about creating a world that carries literary meaning. Design is not just visuals, but a narrative itself.

Besides design, Amy is also an award-winning writer and artist. She has published numerous proses in newspapers, magazines and books and has won several nationwide and citywide writing contests in Taiwan. She is also a Semi-Finalist of the Nikon Storytellers Scholarship in North America.

As a social activist, Amy founded Filmmaking Camp for Teens in rural Taiwan for underprivileged kids and served as the City Director for Symposium Taiwan as part of Symposium Global's efforts to foster cross-cultural dialogues through text. Currently, Amy is pursuing her B.A. in Visual Arts and Sociology at Columbia University.
